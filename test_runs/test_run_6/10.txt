STEP 2: Extracted Transformation Rules

Rule summary
- Preserve each isolated gray input point as a point in the output.
- For every isolated gray point at (r,c) (row=r, col=c) add a blue 3×3 square centered on that point. That square's top-left is (r-1,c-1) and bottom-right is (r+1,c+1).
- The square may be represented either as a single square primitive or decomposed into its border lines and the two diagonals. Concretely the output will contain:
  - point(output,(r,c),gray) for each input isolated_point(input,(r,c),gray)
  - square(output,top_left_corner(r-1,c-1),bottom_right_corner(r+1,c+1),size(3),color(blue))
  - equivalently (if decomposed): horizontal_line at rows r-1 and r+1 from cols c-1..c+1 (length 3), vertical_line at cols c-1 and c+1 from rows r-1..r+1 (length 3), and diagonal_line from (r-1,c-1) to (r+1,c+1) and from (r+1,c-1) to (r-1,c+1).

This rule explains the four 3×3 blue squares centered on the four gray points in grid_1 and the same three centered squares (represented with lines/diagonals) in grid_0.
